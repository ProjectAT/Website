Why Project@

Assessing correctness of students' computer programs is a challenging task. Currently it requires the instructor or a skilled TA to develop a set of special-purpose programs to test students' solutions. This is a highly time-consuming task and the result is typically not easily re-usable. When grading, the TAs need to run these programs, analyze their output, and then examine the students' submissions. The output of these programs may be difficult to read for both students and the TAs, and the quality of feedback that the students receive suffers.

A multi-purpose auto-testing software package will greatly speed up the grading process, thus freeing up valuable TA resources. The freed up TA hours can then be used in direct student-TA contact, thus improving the students' experience and quality of feedback. There are currently a number of exciting initiatives, such as conducting student interviews as part of the grading process, or involving TAs in in-class activities, which are unattainable to the instructors because of the scarcity of TA resources.
